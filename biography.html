Biography

since August 2013   National Center for Smart Growth (NCSG), University of Maryland
Faculty Research Associate

At the NCSG I am responsible for improving the Maryland Statewide Transportation Model (MSTM). Currently, the team is working on the implementation of an auto-ownership model, a synthetic population and a microscopic module to cover trip generation.

Another major project of mine is the implementation of the land use model SILO for Maryland. The model is redesigned to cover location choice at the statewide level. At the same time, SILO is integrated with the travel demand model MSTM to calculate accessibilities, a major driver for household relocation decisions.

During the Spring Semster 2014, I co-tought a class on Research Design for Ph.D. students. In Fall 2014, I co-taught a transportation planning class, where focused on transportation modeling. Currently, I advise one Ph.D. student and several Master Degree students.
2007 - 2013   Parsons Brinckerhoff, Inc.
Principal Professional Associate

At Parsons Brinckerhoff, I had the chance to work on a large variety of project in travel demand modeling, including
- Activity-based models
- Freight models
- Land use models
- Mode choice modeling
- Statewide transportation models
2002 - 2006   Institute of Spatial Planning, University of Dortmund, Germany
Research Assistant and Doctoral Student

While conducting the research for my dissertation, I worked as a research assistant at the Institute of Spatial Planning. My principal research project was the Integrated Land Use Modelling And transportation System Simulation (ILUMASS), the first fully microscopic integrated land-use/transportation model.
1996 - 2002
Department of Spatial Planning, University of Dortmund, Germany
Student, Undergraduate (Vordiplom) and Graduate (Diplom)

During my studies, I worked as a student research assistant at the chair of Sociology for Planners and at the Institute of Spatial Planning. From 2000 to 2001, I spent a year as a Fulbright Scholar at the Department of Urban Design and Planning at University of Washington in Seattle.
